About Me

Hi! I'm Patti. I am a...

Torontonian, Web Developer, U of T Alumni, Visual Artist, Musician, etc.

When I Started

In the summer of 2020 I decided to update my professional skillset and, while trying to decide what I should do, I thought back fondly on my childhood days making Geocities fansites and how I was both good at it and enjoyed it. The idea that I could do something technical, yet creative, was very appealing to me.

And so, I enrolled in a Continuing Education Certificate Course with McMaster University and have been studying and working on projects ever since.

What I Do

I work primarily in front-end development; Designing, developing, and deploying websites from wireframe to an online finished project, like a digital barn raising.

Put more technically, my time spent studying and building things has given me hands on experience in the following:

  • Translating design mock-ups to finished products.
  • Graphic editing software such as Photoshop.
  • Version control systems such as Git.
  • Console/Command-line/Terminal interfaces.
  • Browser testing, debugging, and troubleshooting.
  • Mobile-first, responsive design and best practices.
  • W3C and WCAG international web standards.
  • UX/UI best practices.
  • JavaScript interactivity.
  • Basic Node.js (NPM, JSON).

My style of design is retro and eccentric, muted yet colourful, and a delicate balance between light and dark.

How I Unwind

When I'm at home, you might find me watching creepy videos on YouTube or checking out unidentified bugs on Reddit. I also like watching movies and sitcom reruns, studying (human) languages, and doing puzzles.

Otherwise, I play guitar and spend a lot of my time on music-related things. Right now I'm into 70s rock, but I listen to a little of everything.

I also like to go out and explore what the city has to offer. Going on park walks, going to concerts, and hitting up the movie theatre for retro showings.
